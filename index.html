About Me

I am currently an undergraduate student at Huazhong University of Science and Technology (HUST), majoring in Mathematics and minoring in Computer Science. I have a strong academic record with a GPA of 3.85. My educational journey has also taken me to the University of California, Berkeley, where I was an exchange student, excelling in courses such as Numerical Analysis, Optimization Models, and Several Complex Variables.

My professional experience includes an internship at Shanghai UniDT Technology, where I worked on building a large language model for the insurance sector and developed a text segmentation and visualization module for hierarchical classification. Additionally, I participated in a project at the University of Cambridge, focusing on dynamic states in cortical circuits and optimizing neural network structures. At HUST, I have been involved in research groups exploring differential manifolds, Fourier analysis, and convex analysis theory.

Beyond academics and research, I actively engage in social activities, including organizing university alumni events and performing in drama productions. My contributions to the field of mathematics have been recognized with awards in various mathematical modeling contests.

Research Interests

Keywords: AI Applications, Differential Equations, Algorithm Development

My research interests lie in the fields of artificial neural networks and machine learning. I am particularly passionate about developing algorithms and models that can enhance our understanding and application of these technologies. My work has included the theoretical research and application of convex functions and epigraphs, as well as convolution and partial differential equations in Fourier analysis. I aim to continue exploring these areas and contribute to the advancement of artificial intelligence and its applications.

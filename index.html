About Me

I am currently an undergraduate student at Huazhong University of Science and Technology (HUST), majoring in Mathematics and minoring in Computer Science. I have a strong academic record with a GPA of 3.92. My educational journey has also taken me to the University of California, Berkeley, where I was an exchange student, excelling in courses such as Numerical Analysis, Optimization Models, and Several Complex Variables.

My professional experience includes an internship at Shanghai UniDT Technology, where I worked on building a large language model for the insurance sector and developed a text segmentation and visualization module for hierarchical classification. Additionally, I participated in a project at the University of Cambridge, focusing on dynamic states in cortical circuits and optimizing neural network structures. At HUST, I have been involved in research groups exploring differential manifolds, Fourier analysis, and convex analysis theory.

Beyond academics and research, I actively engage in social activities, including organizing university alumni events and performing in drama productions. My contributions to the field of mathematics have been recognized with awards in various mathematical modeling contests.

Projects

Optimizing the Marker-and-Cell Scheme for Accurate Fluid Dynamics in Lid-Driven Cavity Flow (Huazhong University of Science and Technology)

Duration: February 2024 – May 2024

Role: Research Intern

Description: Investigated the implementation of the Marker-and-Cell (MAC) scheme to effectively model fluid dynamics in Lid-driven cavity flow, focusing on accuracy and computational efficiency in numerical simulations. Analyzed the convergence behavior and stability of the MAC scheme, optimizing parameters to enhance performance and ensure reliable results in simulating complex fluid flow patterns within the cavity.

Integrating Eligibility Traces and Top-Down Signals for Improved Learning in Spiking Neural Networks (Shanghai Jiao Tong University)

Duration: January 2024 – February 2023

Role: Research Intern

Description: Introduced Eligibility Traces to record the contribution of synapses over a period of time to a multi-layer spiking neural network constructed using Leaky Integrate-and-Fire to simulate the spiking mechanism of biological neurons.Introduced Top-Down Learning Signals to carry target information or expected output, guide or adjust the learning process of lower-layer neurons, and helped the network learn more accurately based on feedback signals from higher-level or more complex neural network modules.

Yandao Knowledge Q&A Large Language Model (Shanghai UniDT Technology)

Duration: July 2023 – August 2023

Role: Algorithm Intern

Description: Designed a rule-based recognition method to accurately identify hierarchical structures such as chapters, paragraphs, and clauses by analyzing the tags in the text (such as title style, numbering, and specific key words) and combining it with regular expression matching technology. Introduced a context-aware segmentation algorithm, combining the context information of the sentence, such as the preceding and following words, sentence length, semantic relevance, etc., and solving the problem of fuzzy segmentation boundaries through a machine learning model.

Realistic Seismic Simulation: 3D Modeling of Building Response to Earthquake Dynamics (Huazhong University of Science and Technology)

Duration: March 2023 – March 2024

Role: Research Intern

Description: Designed the 3D model of the building and the physical behavior of each part to ensure the true restoration of the collision effect, by considering the material properties of the building and the friction coefficient, elasticity coefficient and other parameters of the physical material. Designed the frequency, amplitude and direction of longitudinal and transverse waves of seismic waves to realistically reproduce the ground motion in earthquakes of different intensities, based on real earthquake data and using physics engine.

LIF Model: From Theoretical Construction to Application (University of Cambridge)

Duration: August 2022 – September 2022

Role: Research Intern

Description: Designed the simplified Leaky Integrate-and-Fire model to describe the change of neuron membrane potential over time, considering the different weights of the input currents of excitatory and inhibitory neurons respectively to construct neural network. Introduced numerical methods to discretize the differential equations and to calculate the update of the membrane potential at each time step, adjusting the ratio of excitatory neurons to inhibitory neurons, and recording the spike pulse firing frequency of the entire neural network.

Differential Manifolds and Convex Analysis (Huazhong University of Science and Technology)

Duration: January 2022 – April 2023

Role: Research Intern

Description: Analyzed the properties of epigraphs, such as closure and convexity, and their applications in optimization theory such as soft threshold function in L1 regularization and hinge loss in support vector machines to solve practical problems such as image reconstruction and data fitting. Introduced Fourier transform to transform the differential operation in partial differential equation into algebraic operation, simplifying the solution process of PDE and obtaining the solution infrequency domain.
